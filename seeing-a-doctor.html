When to See a Doctor:

  • If you have a temperature over 100.4 Fahrenheit, a new cough, or new shortness of breath, call a doctor.

  • If you are having trouble breathing, chest pains, bluish lips, or abnormal confusion, call 911 or go straight to the nearest ER.

  • If you have symptoms of an emergency unrelated to COVID-19, see a doctor anyway.

  • Do not go to the ER for non-serious symptoms of COVID-19 (sore throat/nausea/loss of taste or smell), though you may want to call your doctor for a COVID-19 test.

  • Do not go to the ER for a COVID-19 test if you are asymptomatic or minimally symptomatic - look for another provider.

How to Stay Safe:

  • If you aren't sure about whether you should see a doctor, call first.

  • If possible, visit your doctor a virtually with an online appointment.

  • If you go to the doctor, make sure to practice good hand washing and social distance where possible.

  • If you go to the doctor, avoid touching shared surfaces and use disinfectant wipes on surfaces you do have to touch.

  • Doctors will often have face masks to give you, but bring your own when you can.

  • If you go to a hospital or an ER, ask the doctor how long you should self-isolate for after the trip.

  • Don't panic. With proper precautions, going to the doctor during this pandemic is very safe.

Fact vs. Fiction:

  • Fiction: It is not safe to visit a hospital during the COVID-19 pandemic.

  • Fact: Hospitals have implemented many safety features to protect staff and patients, so if you think you need to go to the ER you should.

  • Fiction: Most symptomatic people with COVID-19 will be admitted to the hospital.

  • Fact: Most people with COVID-19 will be asked to stay home, so don't go to the hospital unless your symptoms are severe.

  • Fiction: COVID-19 can be effectively beat by herd immunity, with many people getting sick and recovering.

  • Fact: 50-66% of people would need to develop antibodies before herd immunity is effective, and it is unclear how long such antibodies would last. A vaccine is the most effective way to beat COVID-19.
