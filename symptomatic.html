Ways to Stay Safe

  • If you have been exposed to someone who has tested positive for COVID-19 or have symptoms yourself, get tested.

  • Self-quarantine for 14 days after symptoms stop.

  • Seek medical care if symptoms become severe.

  • Inform those who you have been in contact with within the 14 days before dignosis.

  • Take care of yourself, and stay hydrated.

What to Look Out For

  • A fever

  • A cough

  • Trouble breathing

  • Loss of taste or smell

  • If you experience chest pain, severely short breath, blue lips, or abnormal confusion, call 911 or go to the hospital!

Fact vs Fiction

  • Fiction: Catching COVID-19 once makes you immune for life.

  • Fact: COVID-19 antibodies appear to wear off; catching COVID-19 does not necessarily mean you will have it for life.

  • Fiction: If you can hold your breath for 10 seconds without any coughing or discomfort, you don't have COVID-19.

  • Fact: Being able to hold your breath for 10 seconds or more without coughing or feeling discomfort DOES NOT mean you are free from COVID-19

  • Fiction: You can only get COVID-19 over the age of 65.

  • Fact: People of all ages can be infected by the COVID-19 virus, and it can be lethal in all age groups.
